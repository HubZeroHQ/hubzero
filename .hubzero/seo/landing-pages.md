# Landing Pages SEO

Landing pages built for a specific campaign, channel, or paid traffic source optimize for conversion first, but should never work against organic discovery in the process.

---

# Search Intent

Visitors arriving at a landing page typically already have context from an ad, email, or referral. They search, when they search at all, to verify the offer is legitimate and applicable to them before converting.

---

# Primary Keyword Categories

- Offer- and campaign-specific terms
- Audience- or segment-specific phrasing (industry, role, use case)
- Comparative terms if the page targets a specific alternative

---

# Essential Elements

Required

- A single, unambiguous call to action
- Content that matches the promise made in the referring ad or link exactly
- A clear, indexable canonical URL if the page is meant to also rank organically

Recommended

- Social proof relevant to the specific audience segment
- A minimal, fast-loading page free of unrelated navigation that could distract from the one goal

---

# Information Architecture

Decide deliberately whether a landing page is meant to rank organically or exists purely to receive paid/referral traffic. If it is not meant to rank, mark it appropriately (e.g., noindex) rather than letting a thin, narrowly-targeted page compete with — and potentially cannibalize — the site's evergreen content in search results.

If it is meant to rank, avoid creating many near-duplicate landing pages for similar audience segments; consolidate into one strong page with segment-aware content instead of many thin variants.

---

# Structured Data

Recommended

- Product or Offer, where the page represents a specific purchasable offer
- BreadcrumbList only if the page is part of normal site navigation

---

# Content Strategy

Match message to source. A visitor arriving from a specific ad, email, or partner referral should see language that continues that specific conversation, not generic site copy.

Keep the page focused on one action. A landing page trying to convert on three different offers converts on none of them well.

---

# Technical Considerations

Prioritize load speed above nearly everything else — landing pages are disproportionately sensitive to abandonment from slow loads, given the visitor already had to click through from somewhere else. Ensure tracking and analytics tags do not degrade load performance.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Message mismatch between the referring source and the landing page content
- Thin, duplicate landing pages per audience segment cannibalizing each other in search
- Multiple competing calls to action
- Leaving a purely campaign-specific page indexed long after the campaign ends

---

# Success Metrics

Conversion rate, cost-per-conversion by source, bounce rate segmented by traffic source, page load performance.
