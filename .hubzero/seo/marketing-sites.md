# Marketing Sites SEO

Marketing sites — corporate sites, product marketing pages, agency and services sites — build visibility by establishing trust, authority, and a clear understanding of what the business or product does.

SEO should support long-term discovery rather than short-term keyword targeting.

---

# Search Intent

Visitors typically search to:

- Learn what the company or product does
- Understand capabilities and pricing
- Evaluate credibility
- Contact the business or start a trial
- Verify legitimacy

---

# Primary Keyword Categories

- Company or product name
- Industry and category
- Solutions and use cases
- Capabilities and features
- Location, where relevant
- Brand terms

Focus on topical authority rather than individual keywords.

---

# Essential Pages

Required

- Home
- About / Company
- Contact
- Product or Services overview
- Pricing, where applicable

Recommended

- Customer stories or case studies
- Careers
- Resources or blog
- FAQs

---

# Information Architecture

Navigation should communicate:

- Who the company is and what the product does
- Who it serves
- Why it is credible
- How to get in touch or get started

Pages should remain focused and avoid unnecessary overlap.

---

# Internal Linking Strategy

Connect related solutions, case studies, resources, and team or about content. Every important page should be reachable within a few clicks.

---

# Structured Data

Required

- Organization
- Website
- BreadcrumbList

Recommended

- Product
- FAQPage

---

# Content Strategy

Content should demonstrate expertise: case studies, technical articles, product updates, and frequently asked questions. Quality is more important than publishing frequency.

---

# Trust Signals

High priority: real team members, customer testimonials, case studies, certifications.

Medium priority: partnerships, press coverage, integrations.

---

# Technical Considerations

Strong metadata, canonical URLs, an XML sitemap, Open Graph tags, fast loading, mobile-first layout, and accessibility.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Generic marketing copy that could describe any company
- Thin, undifferentiated solution pages
- Duplicate content across similar pages
- Weak or generic About page
- No evidence (case studies, testimonials) backing claims
- Poor internal linking between related content

---

# Success Metrics

Qualified enquiries, organic traffic, branded search volume, conversion rate, time on site.
