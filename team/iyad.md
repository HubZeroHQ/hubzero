# Introduction

I'm an Electronics and Communication Engineering graduate passionate about building products that combine AI, embedded systems, robotics, and modern software.

Beyond engineering, I'm deeply interested in UI/UX, graphic design, branding, and marketing because I believe a great product is defined not only by how it works, but also by how it looks, feels, and connects with people.

My goal is to understand the complete journey of building products—from engineering and design to leadership and execution—so I can contribute to technology that is both technically excellent and genuinely meaningful.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

Technology alone is rarely enough.

Every successful product is the result of multiple disciplines working together: engineering, design, branding, communication, operations, and user experience. Ignoring any one of these weakens the final product.

My approach is built around three simple ideas:

- Build with purpose.
- Design for people.
- Never stop improving.

A product should be technically reliable, visually refined, intuitive to use, and simple enough that people naturally understand it. Engineering should reduce complexity for the user, not expose it.

Rather than specializing narrowly, I chose to understand the entire product lifecycle.

# Projects

## Robotic Arm

**Why it matters**

The robotic arm remains the project I'm most proud of because it was the first time I successfully combined electronics, mechanics, and programming into one working system.

It marked the point where engineering became much more than coursework—it became something I genuinely wanted to pursue.

**Lessons learned**

More importantly, the project taught me that every successful system is built one small problem at a time.

Many parts didn't work the first time, but solving each issue patiently taught me the importance of iteration, experimentation, and learning through hands-on experience.

That mindset continues to shape how I approach engineering today.

# Product Thinking

I've always believed that engineering doesn't stop once the code compiles or the hardware powers on.

A product is the combination of:

- Engineering
- Design
- Branding
- User Experience
- Marketing

Understanding how these pieces fit together allows us to build products that are technically strong while remaining enjoyable and memorable for the people who use them.

The smartphone is one of my favorite examples of this philosophy.
